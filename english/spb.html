Saint-Petersburg

Saint Petersburg (Russian: Санкт-Петербург), formerly known as Petrograd (Петроград) (1914–1924), and later Leningrad (Ленинград) (1924–1991), is a city on the Neva River, at the head of the Gulf of Finland on the Baltic Sea. It is Russia's second-largest city after Moscow. Saint Petersburg is home to the Hermitage, one of the largest art museums in the world, and the Lakhta Center, the tallest skyscraper in Europe.

The city was founded by Tsar Peter the Great on 27 May 1703 on the site of a captured Swedish fortress, and was named after apostle Saint Peter. Saint Petersburg is historically and culturally associated with the birth of the Russian Empire and Russia's entry into modern history as a European great power. The Historic Centre of Saint Petersburg and Related Groups of Monuments constitute a UNESCO World Heritage Site, so it is also referred to as Russia's cultural capital.

Advice

I recommend to visit this city in summer or in spring. In winter, most of the sidewalks are frozen so it is not convenient to visit it even if the city under the snow is very nice. You will need to get a visa if you are not from this country. Having basis in Russian will really be useful. You can have days of 24hours if you go to the city at the good period of the year. In winter, the days are around 5hours, so the visit will be different.
